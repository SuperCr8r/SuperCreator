<!-- ========================= SUMMARY ========================= -->
<section id="summary" class="accordion-section">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-1"><span>Summary</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">

<div class="accordion-subsection">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-2"><span>Overview</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">
<p>Emotional Agility provides a practical framework for engaging with inner experiences—thoughts, emotions, and self-stories—so they inform rather than drive behavior. It emphasizes flexible responding through acceptance, curiosity, and values-guided choices instead of suppression, fusion, or avoidance.</p>
</div>
</div>

<div class="accordion-subsection">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-2"><span>Key Concepts</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">
<ul>
<li>Show up: Recognize and name emotions without judgment.</li>
<li>Step out: Create space between feelings and actions through cognitive distancing.</li>
<li>Walk your why: Clarify values and take small, purposeful steps aligned with them.</li>
<li>Build habits: Use tiny experiments to embed new patterns and increase psychological flexibility.</li>
</ul>
</div>
</div>

</div>
</section>

<!-- ========================= CHAPTERS ========================= -->
<section id="chapter-summary" class="accordion-section">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-1"><span>Chapter-wise Summary</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">

<div class="accordion-subsection">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-2"><span>Introduction — The Need for Emotional Agility</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">
<p>Introduces emotional agility as an alternative to rigid coping strategies. Argues that thriving requires acknowledging inner life and acting with clarity and purpose despite emotions.</p>
</div>
</div>

<div class="accordion-subsection">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-2"><span>Chapter 1 — Showing Up</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">
<p>Teaches skills to notice and name emotions accurately. Emphasizes mindfulness and curiosity rather than judgment or suppression.</p>
</div>
</div>

<div class="accordion-subsection">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-2"><span>Chapter 2 — Stepping Out</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">
<p>Explains how to create psychological distance from thoughts and feelings (cognitive defusion). Offers techniques to observe inner experiences as data, not directives.</p>
</div>
</div>

<div class="accordion-subsection">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-2"><span>Chapter 3 — Walking Your Why</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">
<p>Focuses on clarifying personal values and using them as a compass for behavior. Encourages small, consistent actions that reflect those values.</p>
</div>
</div>

<div class="accordion-subsection">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-2"><span>Chapter 4 — Moving On</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">
<p>Describes turning insights into experiments and habits. Highlights the importance of iteration, self-compassion, and resilience when change is slow or non-linear.</p>
</div>
</div>

<div class="accordion-subsection">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-2"><span>Chapter 5 — Emotional Agility at Work</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">
<p>Applies principles to professional settings: leadership, feedback, and performance. Recommends modeling openness and creating environments that allow emotional honesty.</p>
</div>
</div>

<div class="accordion-subsection">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-2"><span>Chapter 6 — Parenting and Relationships</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">
<p>Shows how emotional agility improves connection by fostering attunement and healthy boundaries. Encourages parents and partners to respond rather than react.</p>
</div>
</div>

<div class="accordion-subsection">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-2"><span>Chapter 7 — When Life Breaks You</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">
<p>Addresses grief, loss, and major transitions. Offers strategies to stay grounded, accept pain, and build meaning in adversity.</p>
</div>
</div>

<div class="accordion-subsection">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-2"><span>Chapter 8 — Cultivating Courage and Curiosity</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">
<p>Encourages experimenting with discomfort and using curiosity to learn from emotions. Presents practices to expand tolerance for difficult feelings.</p>
</div>
</div>

<div class="accordion-subsection">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-2"><span>Chapter 9 — Routines and Rituals</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">
<p>Recommends building simple routines that reinforce values and emotional awareness. Suggests micro-habits that sustain long-term change.</p>
</div>
</div>

<div class="accordion-subsection">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-2"><span>Conclusion — Practice Over Perfection</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">
<p>Reiterates that emotional agility is an ongoing practice. Success comes from consistent, small actions rather than one-off breakthroughs.</p>
</div>
</div>

</div>
</section>

<!-- ========================= EXERCISES ========================= -->
<section id="exercises" class="accordion-section">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-1"><span>Exercises</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">

<div class="accordion-subsection">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-2"><span>Daily Emotional Check-in</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">
<p>Set a daily reminder for a 3-minute check-in. Notice what you're feeling, name it, and note any urges to act. No need to change anything—just observe.</p>
</div>
</div>

<div class="accordion-subsection">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-2"><span>Name It to Tame It</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">
<p>When a strong emotion arises, pause and label it precisely (e.g., "disappointed," "ashamed," "anxious"). Naming reduces intensity and creates space for choice.</p>
</div>
</div>

<div class="accordion-subsection">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-2"><span>Values Clarification</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">
<p>List the top five values that matter most to you (e.g., connection, learning, integrity). For each value, write one small action you can take this week to embody it.</p>
</div>
</div>

<div class="accordion-subsection">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-2"><span>Tiny Experiments</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">
<p>Create a two-week experiment to test a new behavior related to a value. Keep it small, measurable, and educational—focus on learning, not on success/failure.</p>
</div>
</div>

<div class="accordion-subsection">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-2"><span>Self-Compassion Pause</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">
<p>When you notice self-criticism, take a 60-second break: breathe, place a hand over your heart, and speak a compassionate phrase (e.g., "This is hard right now"). Then choose one small compassionate action.</p>
</div>
</div>

</div>
</section>
