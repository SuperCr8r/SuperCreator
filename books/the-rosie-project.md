<section id="tldr" class="accordion-section">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-1"><span>TL;DR</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">

<div class="accordion-subsection">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-2"><span>One-Line Thesis</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">
<p>A brilliant but socially awkward genetics professor uses a logic-driven "Wife Project" to find love, only to have his plan upended by an unpredictable woman, Rosie.</p>
</div>
</div>

</div>
</section>

<!-- ========================= SUMMARY ========================= -->
<section id="summary" class="accordion-section">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-1"><span>Summary</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">

<div class="accordion-subsection">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-2"><span>Brief Summary</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">
<p>Don Tillman, a genetics professor with rigid routines and literal thinking, designs a detailed questionnaire to find a suitable wife. His plan fails when he meets Rosie, who is nothing like his ideal candidate but becomes central to a different project: finding her biological father. Through their collaborative search, Don learns about empathy, flexibility, and love, transforming his understanding of relationships and himself.</p>
</div>
</div>

<div class="accordion-subsection">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-2"><span>Core Themes</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">
<p>Key themes include the tension between logic and emotion, the social challenges of neurodivergence, the unexpected nature of love, personal growth through relationships, and the comedic outcomes of literalism colliding with everyday life.</p>
</div>
</div>

<div class="accordion-subsection">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-2"><span>Main Characters</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">
<p>Don Tillman — Highly logical, socially blunt, and brilliant in genetics. Rosie Jarman — Spontaneous, impulsive, warm, and searching for her father. Gene and Claudia — Friends who offer social guidance. Parents and colleagues provide context and contrast to Don's development.</p>
</div>
</div>

</div>
</section>

<!-- ========================= CHAPTER-WISE SUMMARY ========================= -->
<section id="chapters" class="accordion-section">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-1"><span>Chapter-wise Summary</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">

<div class="accordion-subsection">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-2"><span>Chapter 1 — Don's Routine</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">
<p>Introduction to Don's precise life: schedules, dietary rules, and his decision to develop the "Wife Project" questionnaire to find a compatible partner through measurable criteria.</p>
</div>
</div>

<div class="accordion-subsection">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-2"><span>Chapter 2 — The Questionnaire</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">
<p>Don refines the questionnaire with scientific rigor. He seeks to remove emotional variability and optimize compatibility, revealing his belief that logic can produce a successful relationship.</p>
</div>
</div>

<div class="accordion-subsection">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-2"><span>Chapter 3 — Social Missteps</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">
<p>Don's social awkwardness is shown in interactions that highlight his literalness. He misinterprets social cues, underscoring the gap between his intellectual competence and social understanding.</p>
</div>
</div>

<div class="accordion-subsection">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-2"><span>Chapter 4 — Meeting Rosie</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">
<p>Don meets Rosie at a bar. She fails the questionnaire's criteria but intrigues him. Their chemistry is unpredictable and challenges Don's assumptions about compatibility.</p>
</div>
</div>

<div class="accordion-subsection">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-2"><span>Chapter 5 — The Father Project</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">
<p>Rosie asks Don to help find her biological father. Don agrees, framing it as a scientific investigation. This "Father Project" becomes a catalyst for their deepening connection.</p>
</div>
</div>

<div class="accordion-subsection">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-2"><span>Chapter 6 — Investigation Begins</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">
<p>Don applies systematic methods—records checks, databases, and interviews—to trace Rosie's lineage. His analytical approach contrasts with Rosie's emotional recollections.</p>
</div>
</div>

<div class="accordion-subsection">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-2"><span>Chapter 7 — Mistakes and Humor</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">
<p>Comedic errors arise from Don's inflexible thinking. Misunderstandings amplify the humor but also reveal Don's growing awareness of social dynamics.</p>
</div>
</div>

<div class="accordion-subsection">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-2"><span>Chapter 8 — Growing Closer</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">
<p>Shared experiences during the search build trust. Don begins to notice qualities in Rosie that fall outside his original criteria but resonate personally.</p>
</div>
</div>

<div class="accordion-subsection">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-2"><span>Chapter 9 — Personal Backstories</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">
<p>Both characters reveal vulnerabilities. Rosie's frustrations and Don's childhood history provide context for their behaviors and decisions.</p>
</div>
</div>

<div class="accordion-subsection">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-2"><span>Chapter 10 — Conflict</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">
<p>Tension emerges when Don's proposal for a controlled relationship is rejected by Rosie. Their differing needs lead to arguments and forced introspection.</p>
</div>
</div>

<div class="accordion-subsection">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-2"><span>Chapter 11 — Breakthroughs</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">
<p>Don experiences emotional breakthroughs—small acts of empathy and spontaneity—that indicate change. He begins to value non-logical aspects of life more.</p>
</div>
</div>

<div class="accordion-subsection">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-2"><span>Chapter 12 — Discovery</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">
<p>The investigation uncovers leads and truths about Rosie's past. Revelations force both characters to reassess priorities and expectations.</p>
</div>
</div>

<div class="accordion-subsection">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-2"><span>Chapter 13 — Decisions</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">
<p>Choices about the future come to the fore. Don must decide whether to adhere to his original method or embrace the uncertainty that comes with Rosie.</p>
</div>
</div>

<div class="accordion-subsection">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-2"><span>Chapter 14 — Acceptance</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">
<p>Don accepts complexity and emotional risk. The narrative shows him integrating newfound emotional intelligence with his rational nature.</p>
</div>
</div>

<div class="accordion-subsection">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-2"><span>Chapter 15 — Resolution</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">
<p>The story resolves with a reinterpretation of success: relationships are not problems to be engineered but lived. Don and Rosie's bond endures through mutual growth and compromise.</p>
</div>
</div>

</div>
</section>

<!-- ========================= EXERCISES ========================= -->
<section id="exercises" class="accordion-section">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-1"><span>Exercises</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">

<div class="accordion-subsection">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-2"><span>Discussion Questions</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">
<p>1. Is Don's questionnaire an effective tool for selecting a partner? Why or why not?<br>
2. How does the novel portray neurodiversity—sympathetically, stereotypically, or both?<br>
3. Which scenes best illustrate the conflict between logic and emotion?</p>
</div>
</div>

<div class="accordion-subsection">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-2"><span>Writing Prompts</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">
<p>1. Rewrite a key scene from Rosie's perspective to show contrasts in interpretation.<br>
2. Create an alternative "Wife Project" questionnaire that accounts for emotional intelligence.<br>
3. Write a short epilogue imagining Don and Rosie's life five years later.</p>
</div>
</div>

<div class="accordion-subsection">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-2"><span>Activities</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">
<p>1. Role-play interviews using Don's literal questioning and practice converting answers into open-ended conversation.<br>
2. Map character relationships and how each influences Don's growth.<br>
3. Design a research plan—like Don's—for tracing family history, noting ethical and privacy considerations.</p>
</div>
</div>
