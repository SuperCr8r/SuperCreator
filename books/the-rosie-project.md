<!-- ========================= SUMMARY ========================= -->
<section id="summary" class="accordion-section">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-1"><span>Summary</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">

<div class="accordion-subsection">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-2"><span>Overview</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">
<p>Don Tillman, a brilliant but socially awkward genetics professor, creates a detailed questionnaire to find a perfect wife. The plan derails when he meets Rosie, who is nothing like his criteria: impulsive, unconventional, and on a mission to locate her biological father. Don agrees to help Rosie with her "Father Project," and through a series of unpredictable events, he learns to bend his rules, understand emotional nuance, and fall in love.</p>
</div>
</div>

<div class="accordion-subsection">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-2"><span>Key Themes</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">
<ul>
<li>Rationality vs. Emotion: The tension between Don's analytical worldview and the unpredictable nature of relationships.</li>
<li>Identity and Change: How personal growth often comes from stepping outside routines and comfort zones.</li>
<li>Social Perception: Exploration of neurodiversity, empathy, and the social skills that aren't taught in textbooks.</li>
<li>Love as Transformation: Love as a catalyst that softens rigid behaviors and reveals new priorities.</li>
</ul>
</div>
</div>

</div>
</section>

<!-- ========================= CHAPTER-WISE SUMMARY ========================= -->
<section id="chapters" class="accordion-section">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-1"><span>Chapter-wise Summary</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">

<div class="accordion-subsection">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-2"><span>Chapters 1–2</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">
<p>Introduction to Don Tillman: his routines, literal thinking, and the inception of the Wife Project. Don details his qualities and the stringent criteria he expects from a partner.</p>
</div>
</div>

<div class="accordion-subsection">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-2"><span>Chapters 3–4</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">
<p>Don administers his questionnaire and notes the repetitive failures of dating. He describes his comfortable life and the logic behind eliminating candidates who don't fit his list.</p>
</div>
</div>

<div class="accordion-subsection">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-2"><span>Chapters 5–7</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">
<p>Don meets Rosie, a law student appearing chaotic and incompatible with his criteria. Despite this, an unexpected friendship forms after he agrees to help her find her biological father.</p>
</div>
</div>

<div class="accordion-subsection">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-2"><span>Chapters 8–10</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">
<p>The Father Project begins: Don applies scientific methods to Rosie’s search. Their collaboration reveals Don's analytical strengths and exposes him to messy real-world problems that don't fit his models.</p>
</div>
</div>

<div class="accordion-subsection">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-2"><span>Chapters 11–14</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">
<p>Don's social blind spots lead to awkward but revealing interactions. Rosie challenges Don’s assumptions, and he slowly begins to notice her positive qualities beyond his checklist.</p>
</div>
</div>

<div class="accordion-subsection">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-2"><span>Chapters 15–18</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">
<p>Complications arise when Don’s routines are disrupted. He experiences internal conflict as his logical methods fail to predict emotional outcomes, and small acts of care start to matter more.</p>
</div>
</div>

<div class="accordion-subsection">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-2"><span>Chapters 19–22</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">
<p>Key turning points: Don makes decisions that prioritize Rosie’s needs over his comfort. The pair face challenges that require improvisation rather than planning.</p>
</div>
</div>

<div class="accordion-subsection">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-2"><span>Chapters 23–26</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">
<p>Don reflects on his childhood and previous relationships, offering context for his behavior. He recognizes patterns and contemplates change more deliberately.</p>
</div>
</div>

<div class="accordion-subsection">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-2"><span>Chapters 27–30</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">
<p>The search for Rosie’s father reaches new leads. Don navigates emotional complexity, makes mistakes, and learns the value of apology and flexibility.</p>
</div>
</div>

<div class="accordion-subsection">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-2"><span>Chapters 31–34</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">
<p>Romantic tension grows. Don increasingly interprets his feelings for Rosie and experiments with behaviors outside his comfort zone to support her.</p>
</div>
</div>

<div class="accordion-subsection">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-2"><span>Chapters 35–38</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">
<p>Climax and resolution: revelations about the father search and personal admissions culminate. Don confronts the reality of loving someone who cannot be reduced to a checklist.</p>
</div>
</div>

<div class="accordion-subsection">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-2"><span>Chapters 39–End</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">
<p>Denouement: Don integrates lessons learned, adapts his routines, and accepts unpredictability. The novel closes on a hopeful note of mutual growth and affection.</p>
</div>
</div>

</div>

<button class="accordion-toggle level-1"><span>Exercises</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">

<div class="accordion-subsection">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-2"><span>Discussion Questions</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">
<ul>
<li>Which of Don’s traits are obstacles, and which are strengths? Provide examples from the book.</li>
<li>How does Rosie challenge Don’s worldview, and what does that reveal about empathy?</li>
<li>Is Don on the autism spectrum? How should readers approach discussions of neurodiversity in fiction?</li>
</ul>
</div>
</div>

<div class="accordion-subsection">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-2"><span>Writing Prompts</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">
<ul>
<li>Write a short scene from Rosie’s perspective where she first meets Don and interprets his behavior.</li>
<li>Create an alternate ending where Don reverts to his checklist—explore emotional consequences.</li>
</ul>
</div>
</div>

<div class="accordion-subsection">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-2"><span>Practical Exercises</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">
<ul>
<li>Design your own "project" (like The Wife Project) for a personal goal, then list three ways to incorporate flexibility into it.</li>
<li>For one week, deliberately break a small routine and journal your emotional and practical responses.</li>
