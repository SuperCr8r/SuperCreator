<!-- ========================= SUMMARY ========================= -->
<section id="summary" class="accordion-section">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-1"><span>Summary</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">

<div class="accordion-subsection">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-2"><span>Brief Overview</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">
<p>Ikigai blends Japanese cultural insights with practical advice for finding purpose. The authors explore lessons from Okinawa's longest-lived people, scientific research on flow and longevity, and simple daily practices that support a meaningful life. The emphasis is on small, consistent habits, community, and aligning activity with intrinsic motivation rather than chasing external validation.</p>
</div>
</div>

<div class="accordion-subsection">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-2"><span>Core Components</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">
<p>Core components include: staying active physically and mentally, cultivating social bonds, practicing mindfulness and slow living, engaging in work that fosters flow, and maintaining a balanced diet and rest. The book frames these as practical steps toward discovering and living your ikigai.</p>
</div>
</div>

</div>
</section>

<!-- ========================= CHAPTER-WISE SUMMARY ========================= -->
<section id="chapters" class="accordion-section">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-1"><span>Chapter-wise Summary</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">

<div class="accordion-subsection">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-2"><span>Introduction — What is Ikigai?</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">
<p>Introduces the concept of ikigai, its cultural roots, and how finding a personal ikigai can provide direction and joy. Sets the stage with stories from Okinawa and the idea that purpose can influence longevity and happiness.</p>
</div>
</div>

<div class="accordion-subsection">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-2"><span>Chapter 1 — The Okinawan Secret</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">
<p>Examines the lifestyle of Okinawa's centenarians: strong social networks, purposeful daily routines, light daily exercise, and diets based on whole foods. The chapter highlights community as a key factor for long, healthy lives.</p>
</div>
</div>

<div class="accordion-subsection">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-2"><span>Chapter 2 — Stay Active</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">
<p>Argues against retirement as inactivity. Encourages continued engagement in meaningful tasks and gentle movement. The goal is sustainable activity that keeps body and mind in motion, not extreme exercise.</p>
</div>
</div>

<div class="accordion-subsection">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-2"><span>Chapter 3 — Find Flow</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">
<p>Explores Mihaly Csikszentmihalyi's flow concept: deep focus, intrinsic reward, and loss of self-consciousness during absorbing work. Flow is a pathway to ikigai when work aligns with skills and passion.</p>
</div>
</div>

<div class="accordion-subsection">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-2"><span>Chapter 4 — Start Small</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">
<p>Recommends tiny, consistent habits rather than sweeping life changes. Small daily practices compound over time to produce lasting benefits for health and purpose.</p>
</div>
</div>

<div class="accordion-subsection">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-2"><span>Chapter 5 — Nurture Relationships</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">
<p>Highlights the importance of friends, family, and community groups. Social support provides emotional resilience, shared responsibility, and opportunities to contribute.</p>
</div>
</div>

<div class="accordion-subsection">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-2"><span>Chapter 6 — Diet and Rest</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">
<p>Discusses eating habits from blue zones: moderation, whole foods, and social meals. Also emphasizes regular rest and sleep as pillars of sustained energy and mental clarity.</p>
</div>
</div>

<div class="accordion-subsection">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-2"><span>Chapter 7 — Work and Meaning</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">
<p>Investigates how work can be a source of ikigai. Purposeful work—paid or unpaid—offers structure, community, and opportunities for mastery. The key is alignment with values and skills.</p>
</div>
</div>

<div class="accordion-subsection">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-2"><span>Chapter 8 — Resilience and Acceptance</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">
<p>Addresses coping with setbacks through acceptance, gradual adaptation, and maintaining small routines. Resilience is built through social ties and persistent engagement in meaningful activities.</p>
</div>
</div>

<div class="accordion-subsection">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-2"><span>Conclusion — Living Your Ikigai</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">
<p>Summarizes actionable steps: identify passions and strengths, cultivate community, adopt small daily rituals, and pursue work that enables flow. Encourages experimentation and patience in discovering personal ikigai.</p>
</div>
</div>

</div>
</section>

<!-- ========================= EXERCISES ========================= -->
<section id="exercises" class="accordion-section">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-1"><span>Exercises</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">

<div class="accordion-subsection">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-2"><span>Daily Exercises</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">
<ul>
<li>Ikigai Morning Check — Spend 5 minutes listing one thing you love, one skill you used yesterday, and one way you helped someone.</li>
<li>Mini Movement — 10–15 minutes of gentle movement (walk, stretching, gardening) every day.</li>
<li>Mindful Meal — Eat one meal without screens, focusing on flavors, company, and portion control.</li>
</ul>
</div>
</div>

<div class="accordion-subsection">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-2"><span>Weekly Exercises</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">
<ul>
<li>Flow Session — Schedule a 90-minute block once a week for deep work on a passion project without interruptions.</li>
<li>Social Ritual — Organize or attend a communal activity (tea, walk, club) to strengthen ties.</li>
<li>Reflection Review — Review the week’s activities to note moments of satisfaction and energy drain.</li>
</ul>
</div>
</div>

<div class="accordion-subsection">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-2"><span>Reflection Prompts</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">
<ul>
<li>What activity made me lose track of time this week?</li>
<li>Which small habit gave me the most energy?</li>
<li>Who in my community could I support or ask for support this month?</li>
</ul>
</div>
</div>
