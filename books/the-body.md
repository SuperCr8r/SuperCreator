<!-- ========================= SUMMARY ========================= -->
<section id="summary" class="accordion-section">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-1"><span>Summary</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">

<div class="accordion-subsection">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-2"><span>Overview</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">
<p>The Body is a popular-science exploration of human anatomy and physiology, blending accessible explanations, storytelling, and historical anecdotes. Bryson moves through systems and organs—from skin to brain—highlighting how they develop, function, fail, and are studied. The tone is curious and often humorous, with frequent detours into medical history, scientific personalities, and the limits of human knowledge.</p>
</div>
</div>

</div>
</section>

<!-- ========================= CHAPTER-WISE SUMMARY ========================= -->
<section id="chapters" class="accordion-section">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-1"><span>Chapter-wise Summary</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">

<div class="accordion-subsection">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-2"><span>Chapter 1 — Body by Numbers</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">
<p>An introduction to the human body through surprising statistics—cells, bacteria, weights, and metabolic rates—setting scale and context for later chapters.</p>
</div>
</div>

<div class="accordion-subsection">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-2"><span>Chapter 2 — The Cells</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">
<p>Explains cell structure and function, cellular machinery, DNA basics, and how trillions of cells coordinate to form tissues and organs.</p>
</div>
</div>

<div class="accordion-subsection">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-2"><span>Chapter 3 — Genetic Blueprint</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">
<p>Covers heredity and genetics, the discovery of DNA, how genes guide development, and the interplay of genes and environment.</p>
</div>
</div>

<div class="accordion-subsection">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-2"><span>Chapter 4 — Development and Growth</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">
<p>Follows human development from conception through growth, outlining embryology basics and the stages that shape organs and systems.</p>
</div>
</div>

<div class="accordion-subsection">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-2"><span>Chapter 5 — The Skin</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">
<p>Examines the skin as a protective, sensory, and regulatory organ—its structure, functions, wounds, and the microbes that live on it.</p>
</div>
</div>

<div class="accordion-subsection">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-2"><span>Chapter 6 — Circulation</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">
<p>Describes the heart and blood vessels, how blood transports oxygen and nutrients, and common cardiovascular ailments and advances in treatment.</p>
</div>
</div>

<div class="accordion-subsection">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-2"><span>Chapter 7 — Respiration</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">
<p>Explores lungs, gas exchange, and breathing mechanics, as well as historical efforts to understand and treat respiratory disease.</p>
</div>
</div>

<div class="accordion-subsection">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-2"><span>Chapter 8 — Digestion</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">
<p>Walks through the digestive tract, enzymes, microbiome contributions, and how the gut processes and absorbs food.</p>
</div>
</div>

<div class="accordion-subsection">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-2"><span>Chapter 9 — Nutrition and Metabolism</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">
<p>Discusses calories, nutrients, metabolic regulation, energy expenditure, and modern misunderstandings about diet and health.</p>
</div>
</div>

<div class="accordion-subsection">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-2"><span>Chapter 10 — Muscles and Movement</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">
<p>Details muscle types, how muscles produce movement, the role of tendons and bones, and what stamina and strength depend on.</p>
</div>
</div>

<div class="accordion-subsection">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-2"><span>Chapter 11 — The Skeleton</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">
<p>Covers bone structure, growth, repair, and the skeletal system’s role in protection and mineral balance.</p>
</div>
</div>

<div class="accordion-subsection">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-2"><span>Chapter 12 — The Senses</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">
<p>Surveys vision, hearing, taste, smell, and touch—how sensory organs transduce signals and how the brain interprets them.</p>
</div>
</div>

<div class="accordion-subsection">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-2"><span>Chapter 13 — Brain and Mind</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">
<p>Introduces brain anatomy, neural networks, consciousness questions, memory, and the ongoing mysteries of cognition and behavior.</p>
</div>
</div>

<div class="accordion-subsection">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-2"><span>Chapter 14 — The Immune System</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">
<p>Explains innate and adaptive immunity, how the body defends itself, vaccines, allergies, and autoimmune disorders.</p>
</div>
</div>

<div class="accordion-subsection">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-2"><span>Chapter 15 — Infection and Disease</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">
<p>Looks at pathogens—bacteria, viruses, parasites—historical pandemics, antibiotics, and the arms race between microbes and medicine.</p>
</div>
</div>

<div class="accordion-subsection">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-2"><span>Chapter 16 — Reproduction</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">
<p>Discusses human reproduction, pregnancy, childbirth, fertility, and the biological and social implications of reproductive health.</p>
</div>
</div>

<div class="accordion-subsection">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-2"><span>Chapter 17 — Aging and Repair</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">
<p>Explores cellular senescence, organ decline, mechanisms of repair, and what is known and unknown about extending healthy lifespan.</p>
</div>
</div>

<div class="accordion-subsection">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-2"><span>Chapter 18 — Medical Care and Technology</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">
<p>Surveys advances in diagnostics, surgery, imaging, and pharmaceuticals that have transformed treatment and altered expectations for survival and quality of life.</p>
</div>
</div>

<div class="accordion-subsection">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-2"><span>Chapter 19 — Accidents, Errors, and Limits</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">
<p>Addresses medical mistakes, unknowns in medicine, and how human error and biological complexity set practical limits on outcomes.</p>
</div>
</div>

<div class="accordion-subsection">
<button class="accordion-toggle level-2"><span>Chapter 20 — The Human Condition</span></button>
<div class="accordion-content">
<p>Concludes with reflections on the fragility and tenacity of the body, the value of scientific inquiry, and the ethical questions arising from medical progress.</p>
</div>
</div>

</div>
</section>

<li>Visit a local anatomy museum, medical history exhibit, or virtual anatomy app and compare the models to Bryson’s descriptions.</li>

</ul>
